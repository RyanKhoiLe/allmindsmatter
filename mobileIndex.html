Services

Our Academic Coaches will

  • help you navigate the school system
  • advocate for your teen with school officials
  • check online for assignments
  • ensure that there are no late or missing assignments
  • make frequent assessments of online grades
  • call and email teachers
  • lead student study team meetings
  • strengthen your teen’s executive function

Daily Academic Intervention to

  • focus on core skills and subjects—English, writing, history, math, science, world languages, technology, research
  • prioritize and complete daily homework assignments
  • organize backpack contents during each session
  • study and prepare for tests, quizzes, and presentations
  • monitor and finish long-term projects
  • complete written assignments—including typing, proofreading, editing, and printing
  • inspire the desire for academic success
  • instill confidence, motivation, and resilience
  • encourage effective study habits and organizational skills
  • promote self-advocacy and independent learning
  • establish the relevance of assignments
  • set short and long-term learning goals
  • optimize unique learning styles for effectiveness
  • promote social-emotional development

Parents and Academic Coaches Partner to

  • determine an educational philosophy and goals for teen
  • establish an Academic Learning Plan with expectations and consequences
  • identify areas of academic strength and weakness
  • set up an educational career path for teen
  • navigate the school system, intercede with teachers and administrators at school site
  • advocate for the student during SST, IEP, 504, or other parent-school meetings
  • make frequent face-to-face contact
  • provide telephone and e-mail support

Team

Caitlin Hoffman

President, All Minds Matter

Caitlin holds a Master of Arts Degree in Education Administration and Supervision. She has over twenty years teaching experience at the K-12 grade levels. Caitlin is a Silicon Valley educator and academic intervention specialist who has extensive experience working with students who are gifted and talented (GATE) or who have ADD/ADHD or learning disabilities. She understands the inner workings and complex requirements of the California education system.

Caitlin was raised both in the U.S. and in France, and is bilingual and bicultural with a native proficiency in French. She is the parent of a university student and has seen the school system from both a teacher's and a parent's perspective.
